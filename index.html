About Me

I am a senior Computer Science student-athlete at Georgia Institute of Technology. I have always been passionate about technology and I always wanted to understand how machines, specifically computers work and operate.

I have also been a competetive swimmer for more than 10 years now. I have been to a lot of international competetions all over the world representing my home country Egypt. I was able to attend college in the US and be on an athletic scholarship through swimming. I have competed for Georgia Tech at both the ACC and NCAA level.

My first interaction with programming was during my freshman year when I studied Programming for Engineers using MATLAB, at that time I was planning on studying Aerospace Engineering. After taking my first programming class, I realized how powerful programming is and how I can build a lot of fun projects from scratch without the need of tools and space.

Since then, I have been studying Computer Science and now I am looking forward to extend my knowledge in programming, design and Computer Science fundamentals through an internship or a part-time position in the field of Software Development during the summer or the fall of 2016 respectively.
